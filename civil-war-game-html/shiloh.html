In the first day of the fighting, you work with the cavalry to do reconnaissance work and locate the positions of Union troops. After launching a charge that captures 100 Union prisoners, Forrest is injured and the cavalry retreats.


Dzau Tsz-ze aka Charles Marshall: Brought to Mississippi from Zhejiang by missionaries. Though he was well-liked by comrades, Marshall received similar treatment as slaves while serving in Nathan Bedford Forrest's infamous cavalry unit in battles including Fort Donelson and Shiloh. At the battle of Shiloh, Marshall most likely guarded the prisoners captured in the cavalry charge. The man Marshall served, Captain DC Kelley, afterwards resigned from the army, no longer identifying with the Confederate cause of preserving slavery, and personally believing that the Union should be preserved.
