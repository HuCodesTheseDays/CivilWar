Petersburg

Your regiment has marched to Petersburg with the rest of the Army of the Potomac, and after an initial assault, both armies begin to dig in, making trench lines that would eventually stretch 30 miles long. One day, a Confederate raiding party storms your trench and takes you prisoner, where you are eventually sent to Andersonville prison camp along with 45,000 other Union soldiers. In the camp, you are horribly treated, with over 12,000 of your fellow soldiers dying from disease, malnutrition, and beatings from the guards. Fortunately you survive, and are eventually released at the end of the war.


Ching Lee aka Thomas Sylvanus: Brought to the United States to become educated as a Chinese missionary, Thomas was dissatisfied in the United States and wanted to return to China. At 16 years old, following the outbreak of the war, Thomas enlisted in the 42nd New York Volunteer Regiment, and was a corporal in the Color guard. During the Siege of Petersburg, Thomas was captured and sent to Andersonville prison camp where he contracted a disease that ultimately led him to go blind, all in service to a country that never remembered his sacrifice.
