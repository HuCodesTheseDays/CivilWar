Summary



The decades leading up to the Civil War were some of the most chaotic in American history, as the nation transformed from one composed of farmers and frontiersmen to an economic powerhouse. The industrialization of the North and the growth of the agricultural south resulted in great tension between the two regions. Perhaps the most divisive issue was the institution of slavery, which created deep seated conflicts between the anti-slavery North and the pro-slavery South. This conflict eventually boiled over to become the deadliest war in American history. Despite not necessarily having a vested interest in defending the North, many Asian American soldiers fought for what they thought was right- the abolition of slavery. In the words of Hong Neok Woo: "I volunteered in spite of the advice of my friends against it, for I had felt that the North was right in opposing slavery. My friends thought I should not join the militia and risk my life in war, for my own people and family were in China and I had neither property nor family in America whose defense might serve as an excuse for my volunteering." Our interactive activity seeks to provide people with the opportunity to learn more about these fascinating stories of brave Asian Americans who participated in the Civil War through living out their lives. These soldiers deserve recognition for the sacrifices they made.
