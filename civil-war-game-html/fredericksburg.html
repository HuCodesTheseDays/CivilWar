The battle of Fredericksburg

Following the Battle of Antietam, General McClellan was replaced by General Ambrose Burnside due to his failure to pursue Lee’s army. Subsequently, the Army of the Potomac marched South towards Richmond. The army attempted to cross the Rappahanock river, but delays in getting the necessary pontoon bridges allowed Lee’s army to entrench themselves at Marye’s heights, a strong defensive position. After crossing the river, your regiment is ordered to fix bayonets and to charge the hill. The Confederates fire volley after volley into the lines of your fellow Union troops, who are unable to take the position under heavy fire. At the battle of Fredericksburg, the Union took nearly 13,000 casualties, more than double those of the Confederates, making it a military disaster.


Born in Kwangtung Province, China, Dardelle’s early life is shrouded in mystery. What is known is that he was brought to Connecticut at the age of 7 by Captain David White, where he received his education at Clinton Academy. Dardelle enlisted straight out of school at the young age of 18, and joined the 27th regiment. At the battle of Fredericksburg, Dardelle participated in the charge on Marye’s heights, where he was severely injured in his right shoulder. After the war, Dardelle was honorably discharged and lived out the rest of his life in his hometown of Clinton, where he got married and had 3 daughters. In 1880, he became a naturalized US citizen, but despite his service, was denied his veterans pension due to not knowing his exact date of birth.
