The battle of Antietam

In September of 1862, Robert E. Lee’s Army crossed into Maryland, where they were met by the Army of the Potomac, under the command of General George McClellan. You entered the battle in the early morning on the Union’s right flank, in the middle of a cornfield. Due to the low visibility in the cornfield, you and your regiment are forced into close ranged fighting with the Confederates, firing continuous volleys and oftentimes charging where you participate in hand to hand combat. Eventually, you force the enemy back, but McClellan decides to not pursue them, allowing Lee to escape. The fighting in the Cornfield that day resulted in over 15,000 casualties, adding to the death toll from the bloodiest one day battle in American history. Following the battle, President Abraham Lincoln issued the Emancipation Proclamation, beginning the process of freeing the slaves.


Joseph Pierce: Originally from Canton, China, Pierce’s origins remain unclear. It is believed that he was sold into slavery as a young age and brought to the United States as a child. Pierce enlisted in the army at the age of 20 and joined the Fourteenth Connecticut Infantry regiment. During the battle of Antietam, in addition to his regiment’s heavy casualties Pierce tripped over a fence during the fierce fighting and injured his back. Pierce was forced to withdraw from the fighting and recover at a military hospital for the following year.
