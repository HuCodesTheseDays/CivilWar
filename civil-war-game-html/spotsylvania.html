The battle of spotsylvania

The Army of the Potomac is now marching South, and just a few days after participating in the bloody but indecisive battle of the Wilderness, you find yourself at Spotsylvania Courthouse, a small town where the Confederates are heavily dug in. Your regiment is ordered to charge across open ground against a heavily fortified artillery position called “Bloody Angle”, in almost a day of sustained fighting, your regiment finally pushes through and you manage to capture the position. Unfortunately, Lee’s army manages to retreat, making this battle bloody - but inconclusive once again.


Ching Lee aka Thomas Sylvanus: Brought to the United States to become educated as a Chinese missionary, Thomas was dissatisfied in the United States and wanted to return to China. At 16 years old, following the outbreak of the war, Thomas enlisted in the 42nd New York Volunteer Regiment, and was a corporal in the Color guard. At the battle of Spotsylvania, after the rest of the guard had been killed or wounded, Thomas took up the flag, and kept it flying on the breastworks, an act of bravery for which he was unrecognized.
