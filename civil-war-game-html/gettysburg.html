The battle of Gettysburg

After several more failed Union attacks in the South, General Lee began his second advance into the North, where he was met by the Army of the Potomac at Gettysburg, now commanded by General George Meade, who was appointed just three days before the battle. On July 3rd, the third day of the battle, you participated in the Union’s defense of Cemetery Ridge, which Lee ordered a full scale assault on. During “Pickett’s charge,” the Confederates charged across an open field towards your regiment -which was entrenched behind a stone wall- and were resultantly decimated by rifle and artillery fire, killing or wounding nearly half of the attacking soldiers and forcing them to retreat Following this decisive Union victory battle, Lee was finally forced to retreat from the North and stop his offensive.


Joseph Pierce: Originally from Canton, China, Pierce’s origins remain unclear. It is believed that he was sold into slavery as a young age and brought to the United States as a child. Pierce enlisted in the army at the age of 20 and joined the Fourteenth Connecticut Infantry regiment. During the battle of Gettybsurg, Pierce was one of the first men to join the skirmish line on July 2nd, and during Pickett’s Charge on July 3rd, he volunteered to attack the stone Bliss farm, which was filled with Confederate sharpshooters firing at the Union lines on Cemetery Ridge. Under heavy fire he helped to take the farmhouse, distinguishing himself and earning a rare and impressive promotion to the rank of corporal despite his race.
